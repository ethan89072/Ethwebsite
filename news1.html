Afrobeats: A Decade of Global Influence

By EMZNews | Published: July 2025

The Rise of Afrobeats

Over the last 10 years, Afrobeats has exploded from a continental sound into a global phenomenon. Artists like Burna Boy, Wizkid, Davido, and Tiwa Savage have all played vital roles in pushing the sound beyond African borders.

One of the early turning points came when Wizkid was featured on Drake’s “One Dance”, which hit No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100 and exposed millions to the Afrobeats rhythm for the first time.

Global Recognition and Accolades

Burna Boy's Twice As Tall album won the Best Global Music Album at the Grammy Awards in 2021, cementing Afrobeats' position on the world stage. Since then, global media like Rolling Stone, Pitchfork, and Billboard have spotlighted Afrobeats as a rising cultural force.

Meanwhile, Davido’s Timeless album broke multiple streaming records, proving the demand is real. The genre’s influence can be seen in pop, hip-hop, and even K-pop collaborations.

Streaming Numbers Don't Lie

  • Wizkid: Over 5 Billion+ streams on Spotify
  • Burna Boy: Most streamed African artist globally
  • Davido: First African artist to perform on the FIFA World Cup stage (2022)

These numbers tell the story: Afrobeats isn't just a vibe — it's a movement.

Cultural Impact Beyond Music

From fashion to slang to social media trends, Afrobeats artists are now cultural influencers. Phrases like “she tell me say” or “E choke” have made their way into global lingo. Afrobeats isn't just something people listen to — it’s how people live.

What’s Next for Afrobeats?

With rising stars like Asake, Tems, Ayra Starr, and Rema taking charge, the future of Afrobeats is looking unstoppable. Tems' live performances and Rema’s Billboard Hot 100 entry prove that Afrobeats has the ability to evolve while staying true to its African roots.

Conclusion

This is more than music. Afrobeats is shaping culture, shifting conversations, and building bridges. It's time the world recognized that the next global empire isn’t coming — it’s already here. And it's got drums, rhythm, and fire vocals.
